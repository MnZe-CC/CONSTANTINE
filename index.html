A Journey Through Time

Constantine's history spans over two millennia, making it one of the oldest and most significant cities in North Africa.

203 BC

Cirta - Numidian Capital

Originally called Cirta, the city served as the capital of the Berber kingdom of Numidia. In 112 BC, it was the capital of King Jugurtha.

313 AD

Renamed After Constantine

After being destroyed in 311 AD during civil war, the city was rebuilt and renamed "Constantina" in honor of Emperor Constantine the Great.

1529

Ottoman Era

The city became part of the Ottoman Empire, ruled by a Turkish bey. Salah Bey (1770-1792) greatly embellished the city with much of the Islamic architecture still visible today.

1837

French Colonization

After fierce resistance led by Ahmed Bey, the city was captured by French forces. It remained under French rule until Algeria's independence in 1962.

2015

Arab Capital of Culture

Constantine was named the Arab Capital of Culture, celebrating its rich heritage and contributions to Arab and Mediterranean civilization.

The City of Bridges

Constantine's dramatic topography—a rocky plateau divided by the deep Rhumel River gorge—necessitated the construction of magnificent bridges that have become symbols of the city.

🌉

Sidi M'Cid Bridge

168m suspension bridge • Opened 1912

The most iconic and highest bridge in Constantine. A stunning suspension bridge offering breathtaking views of the gorge below.

🏛️

Bab El Kantra Bridge

Historic bridge • Built 1792

The oldest surviving bridge, leading north from the city. A testament to Ottoman engineering excellence.

🏗️

Sidi Rached Viaduct

447m viaduct • 27 arches • 1912

A magnificent viaduct designed by Paul Séjourné, featuring 27 elegant arches spanning the gorge.

🚶

Mellah Slimane Bridge

Pedestrian bridge • With elevator

A modern pedestrian bridge featuring a glass elevator that descends into the gorge, offering unique perspectives of the city.

💧

Bridge of the Falls

Arched bridge • Built 1925

A picturesque series of arches built atop a waterfall, creating one of the most romantic spots in the city.

🛤️

Salah Bey Viaduct

Modern cable-stayed bridge

A stunning modern addition to the city's bridge collection, named after the famous Ottoman ruler who beautified Constantine.

Landmarks & Monuments

From ancient ruins to Ottoman palaces, Constantine offers a wealth of historical and cultural sites to explore.

🕌

Emir Abdelkader Mosque

A magnificent modern mosque, one of the largest in Algeria, serving as both a place of worship and a university.

🏰

Ahmed Bey Palace

The 19th-century palace of the last Ottoman bey, showcasing traditional Algerian architecture and beautiful gardens.

🏛️

Cirta Museum

Formerly the Gustave Mercier Museum, housing an impressive collection of ancient and modern Algerian art and artifacts.

📿

Great Mosque of Constantine

A historical mosque dating back to 1136, one of the oldest religious buildings in the city.

🗿

Monument aux Morts

A WWI memorial monument honoring those who lost their lives, featuring impressive architecture and panoramic views.

🏺

Tiddis Ruins

The ancient Roman city of Tiddis, located nearby, offers a glimpse into the region's Roman past with well-preserved ruins.

🧱

Roman Aqueduct

Impressive ruins of the Antonian Roman aqueduct that once supplied water to the ancient city.

📚

Municipal Library

A beautiful historic building housing a significant collection of manuscripts and books, reflecting the city's intellectual heritage.

Culture & Traditions

Constantine's rich cultural heritage reflects its diverse history, from Berber and Arab traditions to Ottoman and Mediterranean influences.

🎨 Arts & Crafts

Constantine is renowned for its metalwork, particularly silver jewelry and decorative items. The city's artisans continue centuries-old traditions of intricate craftsmanship, producing beautiful filigree work and ornate pieces that are sought after throughout Algeria.

🍽️ Cuisine

The city's signature dish is Chakhtchoukha, a traditional stew made with torn flatbread (ragout), lamb, and aromatic spices. Other specialties include Lham Lahlou (sweet lamb dish) and various couscous preparations.

🎵 Music

Constantine has a rich musical heritage, particularly Malouf, a form of Andalusian classical music that was recognized by UNESCO as intangible cultural heritage. The city hosts music festivals celebrating this ancient art form.

🏛️ Architecture

The city showcases a unique blend of architectural styles: ancient Roman ruins, Ottoman-era mosques and palaces, French colonial buildings, and modern structures. The Casbah (Swika) preserves traditional urban architecture.

📚 Intellectual Heritage

As the Arab Capital of Culture 2015, Constantine has a strong intellectual tradition. It's home to several universities and has produced notable scholars, writers, and artists throughout its history.

🌳 The Kasbah

Known locally as Swika, the historic Kasbah quarter features narrow winding streets, traditional houses, and historic mosques. It offers an authentic glimpse into the city's Ottoman past.

🌍 Location

Northeastern Algeria, 80 km from the Mediterranean coast

Coordinates: 36°21'N 6°36'E

Elevation: 694m above sea level

🌤️ Climate

Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers and mild, moist winters

Best time to visit: Spring (March-May) and Fall (September-November)

✈️ Getting There

Mohamed Boudiaf International Airport serves the city with connections to major Algerian and international destinations.

Trains and buses connect Constantine to Algiers, Oran, and other major cities.

🏨 Where to Stay

Constantine offers a range of accommodations from traditional guesthouses in the Kasbah to modern hotels with views of the famous bridges.
